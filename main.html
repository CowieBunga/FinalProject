What is Artificial Intelligence?

According to Merriam Webster, AI is "an area of computer science that deals with giving machines the ability to seem like they have human intelligence." Basically, the programmers (us) try to make a computer smart enough to make decisions and think logically on their own.

What is Space Exploration?

Space Exploration is the discovery and investigation of celestial structures in outer space, involving technology. Throughout my website, I will talk about how exploration has been made increasingly easier by the development of AI and how the future will be impacted by space and AI.

Examples

According to SingularityHub, AI has made scientific research and space exploration 10 times easier. There are already many AI powered machines in space right now that are helping humans down on earth.

Earth Observer 1 (EO-1) satellite was launched in the early 2000s and its onboard AI systems helped optimize analysis of and response to natural occurrences, like floods and volcanic eruptions.

CIMON (Crew Interactive Mobile CompaniON) is a mobile and autonomous machine that is meant to help astronauts with everyday tasks on the International Space Station (ISS). It landed on the ISS on July 2nd 2018. CIMON was programmed to create a feeling of talking to a crew mate as to simulate the most authentic interaction possible.

The Next Step: Mars

A Brief Overview

Human colonization of Mars is something that is becoming more real everyday. When Earth and Mars are closest, the trip would take 260 days. The Mars 2020 rover is in intense development, and has doubled in productivity because of AI.

Current Limitations

1.

Despite AI's productivity factor, there are still many problems with inhabiting Mars. The main problem is that it would get extremely claustrophobic. The atmosphere is too thin, and the planet isn't warm enough to sustain human life. This would mean that people would have to live in environment controlled infrastructure, that should be able to sustainably produce food and water.

2.

If we want to learn more about Mars, the answer is simple: we need to make better AI. The main problem right now is the communication delay. A rover on Mars solely controlled by a team of engineers can only be given instructions to move every 20 minutes. If we are going far out in space, AI machines need to be smart enough to make decisions on their own.

3.

Computer processing power is also an issue. Due to radiation risk, the average desktop is likely to be stronger than another going into space in the near future. If scientists figure out a way to reduce radiation, the future of space exploration would skyrocket (pun intended)

A machine called the Square Kilometer Array is in development right now and is proposed to produce around the same amount of data that is currently on the internet in a day. It would gather super high quality celestial images, and find correlations between them, allowing scientists to make discoveries. AI innovations like this have made data processing extraordinarily efficient.

How Will AI Affect

Ethics, The World and Jobs?

In 2015, Elon Musk, Stephen Hawking and other science and tech leaders signed an open letter saying that artificial intelligence technology could generate a global arms race unless the United Nations steps in with a ban. Their theory is that if military powerhouses like the US or Russia begin mass production of AI weapons, an arms race is inevitable. Clearly, there are concerns being expressed, but leading scientists believe AI shouldn't be thought of killing machines, rather a step in the right direction towards a more advanced future.

Just a year ago, Musk noted in a documentary that AI research might create an "immortal dictator from which we could never escape". In 2014, Hawking said artificial intelligence might end up annihilating the human race.

Will AI Take Over Our Jobs?

A survey by Pew Research finds Americans are roughly twice as likely to express worry (72%) than enthusiasm (33%) about a future of AI in the workplace. AI can do basic tasks that are monotonous and repetitive, while humans work on more advanced tasks. AI can do these jobs more efficiently and effectively as well.

Jobs likely to be taken over: Concierges, Truck Drivers and Retail Managers.

Jobs unlikely to be taken over: Massage therapists, Animal Scientists, Archaeologists

People need to focus on the bigger picture: how to redesign jobs as a whole and change business processes. AI can make the physical workers more productive by letting them focus on the tasks that need more attention. Just because some of the activities in a job have been automated, does not imply that the whole job has disappeared.

The Sky Image Cataloging and Analysis Tool (SKICAT) has helped astronomers identify objects in space that humans wouldn't be able to do otherwise. Similar machines have helped discover new gravitational lenses that play a crucial role in connection with dark matter. This type of technology is especially exciting because it can also be used to see if a planet's geographical features are suitable for life. AI can make research and celestial identification much easier. It would be obscene to make humans do this kind of thing. With AI doing the jobs like this, humans can focus on the exploration side of things.

A Personal Opinion

With AI taking jobs that are mindless, humans will be able to focus on tasks that nurture innovation, which, in turn will benefit society as a whole. AI taking over easy jobs will encourage more people to pursue higher education, which leads to a more nourished economy. In the short term, there may be some frictional and structural unemployment, but in the long term, AI will help humans flourish, both in space and on land.
